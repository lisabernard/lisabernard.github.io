About Me

I am a very dedicated and hard-working individual, but I didn’t start out that way. In fact, I almost failed grade primary. Yes, you read that right. I wasn’t doing any of the work, off in my own little world. Fortunately, that quickly changed.

During my time in Elementary school, I won “student of the month” twice. It is an award that recognizes exceptional students. One is awarded to a student in each grade level.

In grade 5, the New Glasgow News hired me to deliver 50 papers each morning before school. I was constantly complemented on my dedication, in fact I still have all the Christmas cards I received during my time there. In my grade six graduation celebration, I was awarded the “Eric Doucette Award” for Excellence in Math. On the last day of grade six, I was presented a medal for sportsmanship. Earlier that year I signed up for the 800-meter run and came in second last. I was just happy to place.

I later went on to earn the “medal for exceptional effort” in grade eight. It is awarded once a year to a single student in each grade who is the most studious.

I found out about the Quebec school trip in my last year of middle school and it costed $1000 to go. I got another paper route job, this time for the weekly news delivering 150 fliers, once a week. Over the course of 4 months, I saved up the money to go.

I started High School in the IB (International Baccalaureate) program. It taught me some great study and homework habits. In grade 11, I left the program and entered the main stream, due to the crushing amount of homework. I would begin nightly, about half an hour after I got home, and spend easily three hours straight on it. Since I was taking Karate at the time, I could not afford to procrastinate.

I was thinking about pursing a career in IT. I had a Co-op placement at Staples installing windows, removing hard drives and RAM. I also took Java and quickly found, I liked working with hardware over software. I graduated High School with honours, all my teachers loved me.

I then took a year off to save up for school. I got a Job at ToysRus stocking shelfs on the night shift. It was quite a change since unlike most teens, I didn’t stay up late. In fact, before my first day, I had never stayed up later than 2:30am. I adapted, and put away enough to resume school.

In September 2016, I started my Computer Electronics Course. Python was one of my first semester courses and while learning if statements, my teacher compared them to choices in a 'create your own adventure' game. This originally inspired me to create an Invader Zim ‘choose your own story’ game. Three weeks into development, I switch over from Python if statements to individually linked webpages for compatibility sake. Since then, I released a demo version of the game to the public.
